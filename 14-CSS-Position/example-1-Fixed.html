Beyond that threshold, the element would be fixed to 10 pixels from the top. A common use for sticky positioning is for the headings in an alphabetized list. The "B" heading will appear just below the items that begin with "A" until they are scrolled offscreen. Rather than sliding offscreen with the rest of the content, the "B" heading will then remain fixed to the top of the viewport until all the "B" items have scrolled offscreen, at which point it will be covered up by the "C" heading, and so on. You must specify a threshold with at least one of top, right, bottom, or left for sticky positioning to behave as expected.
